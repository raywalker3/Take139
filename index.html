A NOTE FROM DR. CHRISTOPHER HILKEN ABOUT THE ASSESSMENT

You have been through a lot in your life. I don't need to have met you to know that to be true. There are a seemingly infinite number of things that have happened to you — decisions you have made, losses experienced, moments of betrayal. These events begin to construct a certain set of natural responses that we have in life. Some of these responses are helpful, some not so much.

This whole assessment is built on thousands of hours of peer-reviewed study, surveys, and focused attention on those responses. The knowledge of these loops we find ourselves in is meant to bring understanding and ultimately, through the grace of God, the ability to better recognize these patterns and achieve healthier relationships.

Three things to note as you begin.

  1. You are not a static individual. You are complex and multi-faceted. In discovering some language that puts words to your experiences, you are not bound by those things. Every personality trait has a helpful side and a shadow side. It's not better to be one type of person than another. As scripture tells us in Romans 12, we are all members of a body that Christ has uniquely called and equipped us to be engaged in. It's not better to be outgoing than quiet and introspective — but both types will have different skills and potential pitfalls.
  2. This isn't an online guess based on arbitrary trivia. We have all, likely, taken a “Which cast member of Friends would you be?” assessment before — where you tell the app your birthday and your favorite food and it guesses, from a few random data points, the totality of your personality. Take139 is built to synthesize your self-reported answers into digestible concepts and language you can look through. It isn't guessing what you could be — it's summarizing what you have reported. There will certainly be things that you may read that you wouldn't self-ascribe in any obvious way, but it is how you answered the prompts. Sometimes it's helpful to run that by someone close to you, and you will often find it may be truer than you think.
  3. This isn't scripture, the Holy Spirit, your local pastor, or a personal counselor — and it's no substitute for any of those things. It's a helpful tool. It isn't a box you must live in or a limit you can't supersede. It's a look into your world through a lens that hopefully brings clarity and sheds light on some truth that we can process.

Know yourself before you make a vow

Every couple fights. But most couples don't understand why they fight. Not the surface reason — the story underneath. The old wound that got reopened. The question you've been asking since childhood that your partner didn't know they were answering.

This assessment is a four-part diagnostic that helps you name what most people spend years in marriage discovering the hard way: your triggers, your core wound, the way you protect yourself, and what happens when the protection fails.

The result is a single, complete sentence — your conflict origin story — that you can hand to your partner and say: "This is what's really happening when we fight."

It replaces accusation with understanding. It replaces "you always do this" with "when I feel this, I am fighting an old battle you didn't start."

How it works

The diagnostic moves from outside to inside and back to outside — tracing the path that conflict actually takes through your nervous system and your story.

1

Your Triggers

What gets through your defenses — the specific kinds of pain you feel faster and deeper than others.

2

Your Core Question

The wound underneath — the question you've been asking since long before this relationship started.

3

Your Mechanism

How you protect yourself — the system you built to keep your core question from being answered the wrong way.

4

Your Breakdown

What happens when the wall fails — the pattern that emerges when someone gets through your defenses.

Your complete sentence

At the end, you'll receive a personalized conflict origin sentence — filled in with your specific results:

"I am ______ and I grew up in a home that was ______ and ______. You will find that I'm mainly bothered when I experience ______. The reason this triggers something deep in me is because an intrinsic question I'm constantly asking myself is 'Am I ______?' I protect myself from feeling this way most of the time by being ______. But when something gets through my defenses, I cope with that pain by becoming ______."

Your complete profile also includes personalized behavioral markers, gospel truths specific to your pattern, and couple discussion questions for you and your partner.
