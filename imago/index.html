IMAGO. The deeper profile.

Take 139 names the loop you fall into under pressure. IMAGO names the person God made underneath it.

What IMAGO will be

IMAGO is the longer-form companion to Take 139, currently in development. Where Take 139 is a focused diagnostic — the trigger, the question, the mechanism, the breakdown — IMAGO is a fuller portrait of how God designed you to bear his image in this particular life. It will be a paid assessment of comparable depth to MBTI or the Enneagram, but built from the ground up on Christian theological anthropology rather than secular personality theory.

The assessment is being built carefully, with peer review, pastoral oversight, and a slower release cadence than Take 139. It is not finished yet. We are not in a hurry to put it in front of you before it is ready.

How IMAGO differs from Take 139

Take 139

The Conflict Loop

A focused diagnostic. What happens to you under relational pressure — the trigger, the question, the mechanism that protects you, the breakdown when it collapses. Practical, fast, action-oriented.

IMAGO

The Whole Person

A fuller theological portrait. Who God made you to be when you are running on the gospel rather than on protection. Vocation, gifting, weakness, growth — read through the lens of the imago Dei.

The doctrine underneath it

The whole assessment is built on one of the oldest and most important convictions in Christian theology: that every human being is made in the image of God (Genesis 1:26–27). The imago Dei is not erased by the Fall. It is wounded, distorted, sometimes nearly buried — but never destroyed. The redemption Christ accomplishes is not the manufacture of a new image but the restoration of the one already there.

"Then God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness… So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them."

— GENESIS 1:26–27

Most modern personality assessments treat the human person as a bundle of preferences, traits, or behavioral patterns. They produce something usable — a label, a type, a category — but their theological anthropology is thin or absent. IMAGO begins where Christian doctrine begins: with the conviction that you were intentionally designed, that the design has been distorted by sin, and that the work of the Spirit is to restore the design rather than replace it.

What you will receive when it ships

01

A profile of design

Your unique configuration of strengths, weaknesses, and natural inclinations — read as evidence of how God shaped you, not as a verdict.

02

A profile of distortion

The specific ways your design has been bent by the Fall, by formative experience, by repeated patterns of self-protection.

03

A path of restoration

Scripture, practices, and pastoral counsel calibrated to how the gospel restores your shape — not a generic Christian growth plan.
